## Section 3: Step-by-Step Renewal/Reactivation Guide

### For Active License Renewal (7 Steps)

**Step 1: Monitor Your Timeline**
- Know your exact expiration date
- Understand that TULIP opens 90 days before expiration
- Set calendar reminders early

**Step 2: Complete Required In-Service Education**
- 4 hours: Annual Infection Prevention & Control
- 4 hours: Geriatric Care Fundamentals
- 4 hours: Dementia & Alzheimer's Care Essentials
- 12 additional hours: Any approved courses
- Total: 24 hours

**Step 3: Gather Your Documentation**
- Collect original CEU certificates
- Prepare employer verification (if needed)
- Verify contact information is current
- Gather Social Security Number for verification

**Step 4: Wait for TULIP Window to Open**
- TULIP opens exactly 90 days before expiration
- You cannot renew before this date
- Receive automatic notification from Texas HHSC

**Step 5: Submit Renewal Through TULIP**
- Log into TULIP portal at https://www.tulip.texas.gov
- Verify personal information
- Upload CEU documentation
- Confirm required topics coverage
- Submit application and pay fee ($75-$100)

**Step 6: Monitor Status & Respond to Requests**
- Check TULIP portal for status updates
- Respond immediately to any deficiency notices
- Provide additional documentation if requested

**Step 7: Receive New License**
- New license available in TULIP portal immediately upon approval
- Physical license arrives by mail within 1-2 weeks
- Update new expiration date in your records

### For Expired License Reactivation (8 Steps)

**Step 1: Understand Your Situation**
- Confirm exact expiration date
- Understand employment implications
- Verify Texas regulations

**Step 2: Determine Your Path**
- Contact Texas HHSC for guidance
- Expired < 2 years: Standard renewal
- Expired > 2 years: Reinstatement (more complex)

**Step 3: Complete Updated In-Service Education**
- All 24 hours must be current (recent courses preferred)
- Same requirements as active renewal:
  - 4 hours Infection Control
  - 4 hours Geriatrics
  - 4 hours Dementia
  - 12 additional hours

**Step 4: Verify Employer Status**
- Notify current/prospective employer
- Provide CEU copies
- Prepare Form 5506-NAR if required

**Step 5: Access TULIP for Renewal/Reinstatement**
- Log into TULIP at https://www.tulip.texas.gov
- Select appropriate application type
- Upload current CEU documentation

**Step 6: Submit Application & Fee**
- Complete application form
- Pay renewal fee ($75-$100)
- Submit with all required documentation

**Step 7: Monitor Status & Follow Up**
- Check TULIP daily for first month
- Address any requests immediately
- Follow up if no response after 2 weeks

**Step 8: Receive Reactivated License**
- License available in TULIP upon approval
- New 24-month validity period begins
- Can resume work as CNA immediately

## Section 5: TULIP Information & Official Links

### TULIP Portal Overview

**What is TULIP?**
- Texas Uniform License and Permit System
- Official online portal for license renewal/reactivation
- Managed by Texas Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC)

**Access TULIP:**
- 🔗 **Portal:** https://www.tulip.texas.gov
- 🔗 **Support:** https://www.tulip.texas.gov/support
- 🔗 **CNA Registry:** https://hhs.texas.gov/nurses-aids

### What You Need for TULIP

**Personal Information:**
- Texas CNA Certificate Number or Social Security Number
- Date of Birth
- Current contact information (address, phone, email)

**CEU Documentation:**
- Certificates from all 24-hour courses
- Provider names and course dates
- Proof of required topics (infection control, geriatrics, dementia)

**Employer Verification (if applicable):**
- Form 5506-NAR if required
- Employer/facility name and license number
- Director of Nursing contact info

**Payment:**
- Credit or debit card for renewal fee ($75-$100)

### Texas HHSC Contact Information

**Phone:** 512-438-1234
**Email:** contactcna@dshs.texas.gov
**Website:** https://hhs.texas.gov/nurses-aids
**Chat Support:** Available through TULIP portal

### TULIP Timeline

1. **Now:** Complete your 24-hour CEU requirement
2. **90 Days Before Expiration:** TULIP window opens
3. **First Day Window Opens:** Log in immediately, verify information
4. **Within 2 Weeks:** Upload all CEU certificates
5. **Within 3 Weeks:** Confirm required topics, submit application
6. **5-10 Business Days:** Processing
7. **Upon Approval:** New license available (mail + digital)
8. **Before Expiration:** Download/print for your records

## Section 6: Frequently Asked Questions

### Active License Renewal FAQs

**Q: Can I renew my license early?**
A: No. TULIP only opens 90 days before your license expires. You cannot renew before that window.

**Q: What if I don't complete 24 hours before the TULIP window opens?**
A: You can still renew if you complete them by the TULIP deadline. TULIP allows you to add documents as you submit them.

**Q: Can I renew after my license expires?**
A: Not through standard renewal. After expiration, you must file a Reinstatement application (takes longer, more requirements).

**Q: What if TULIP is down on the deadline day?**
A: Contact Texas HHSC immediately. You may receive an extension if there are system issues.

**Q: Do I need to be employed to renew?**
A: No. Your employment status is separate from renewal.

**Q: If I'm employed at a facility, do they file the renewal?**
A: No. You file through TULIP. Your employer may provide verification if needed.

**Q: What if my CEU was from out of state?**
A: Texas generally requires Texas-approved providers. Verify with HHSC if your course qualifies.

**Q: How long does processing take?**
A: Usually 5-10 business days. You'll receive email updates.

**Q: Can I download my license before it's mailed?**
A: Yes! Digital certificate is available immediately in TULIP upon approval.

**Q: What's the renewal fee?**
A: Current fee is $75-$100. Exact amount displays in TULIP.

### Expired License Reactivation FAQs

**Q: How long has my license been expired?**
A: Check your expiration date. < 2 years = easier process; > 2 years = may need reinstatement.

**Q: Will I lose my seniority?**
A: No. Your prior work history remains on record.

**Q: Do I need a job to reactivate?**
A: No. You can reactivate without a job, but employers may verify active status.

**Q: Do I need to retake the CNA exam?**
A: No. Reactivation only requires updated CEU documentation.

**Q: Can I work while reactivation is pending?**
A: No. You cannot work until officially reactivated in TULIP.

**Q: Will reactivation cost more?**
A: Same basic fee ($75-$100), though reinstatement may have extra fees.

**Q: How long does reactivation take?**
A: 5-10 business days (renewal) or 2-3 weeks (reinstatement) if complete.

**Q: What if I lost my certificate number?**
A: Use your Social Security Number instead. TULIP will look up your record.

**Q: Can my employer help with reactivation?**
A: They can provide Form 5506-NAR verification, but you must submit through TULIP.

**Q: What if my information changed?**
A: Update in TULIP. You may need verification for legal name changes.

## Best Practices for Successful Renewal/Reactivation

### ✅ DO's

- ✓ Complete courses throughout your license period (don't wait until the end)
- ✓ Collect and organize certificates as you finish each course
- ✓ Monitor your TULIP window opening date
- ✓ Log into TULIP immediately when the window opens
- ✓ Review your information carefully before submitting
- ✓ Save your TULIP confirmation number
- ✓ Contact Texas HHSC if you have questions
- ✓ Print/download your digital license immediately upon approval
- ✓ Update your calendar for the next renewal (24 months out)

### ❌ DON'Ts

- ✗ Don't wait until the last day of the TULIP window to submit
- ✗ Don't use CEUs from non-approved providers
- ✗ Don't submit without all required documentation
- ✗ Don't use courses from before your license period
- ✗ Don't assume your employer will submit for you
- ✗ Don't work after your license expires
- ✗ Don't forget that annual infection control is required every single year
- ✗ Don't ignore deficiency notices from TULIP
