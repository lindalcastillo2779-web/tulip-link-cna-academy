# Chapter 7: Elimination and Catheter Care

## Learning Objectives
- Assist with toileting safely and with dignity
- Measure and record urinary output accurately
- Provide catheter care and prevent infections
- Recognize changes in elimination patterns
- Handle bowel and bladder care professionally
- Follow facility protocols for specimen collection

---

## 7.1 Toileting Assistance
Help residents use the bathroom with respect.

Steps include:
- Provide privacy and a call signal
- Offer assistance to the toilet or commode
- Support proper hygiene after elimination
- Encourage independence when safe
- Observe for difficulty or discomfort

Remember to keep the resident covered and comfortable.

---

## 7.2 Urinary Output Measurement
Accurate measurement is important for monitoring health.

To measure output:
- Put on gloves
- Pour urine into a clean graduate at eye level
- Read the volume from the lowest line
- Record the exact amount, not an estimate
- Clean and store equipment properly

Report unusual color, odor, or decreased output.

---

## 7.3 Catheter Care
Catheter care helps prevent infection and keeps the system flowing.

Guidelines:
- Keep the catheter secure and free of kinks
- Keep the drainage bag below bladder level
- Clean the catheter where it exits the body
- Never tug or pull on the tubing
- Change gloves between steps and wash hands after

Comfort and safety are the primary goals.

---

## 7.4 Bowel Care and Incontinence
Support normal elimination and dignity.

Include:
- Assistance with commodes or bedpans
- Gentle perineal care after bowel movements
- Use of absorbent products when ordered
- Encouragement of regular toileting schedules
- Reporting constipation or diarrhea

Consistent care prevents skin irritation and discomfort.

---

## 7.5 Specimen Collection
Some tests require clean catch or timed specimens.

General principles:
- Use the correct container
- Label with resident name and date/time
- Follow instructions for special specimens
- Avoid contamination
- Deliver specimens promptly as directed

Careful handling protects test accuracy.
