# Chapter 10: Rest and Sleep

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the importance of sleep and rest
- Create a comfortable sleep environment
- Support residents with sleep difficulties
- Recognize factors affecting rest
- Provide calm late-night care
- Promote healthy sleep routines

---

## 10.1 The Role of Sleep
Sleep supports physical and mental health.

Good rest helps with:
- Healing and recovery
- Memory and mood
- Immune system function
- Energy and alertness

Disturbed sleep can affect behavior, appetite, and safety.

---

## 10.2 Creating a Restful Environment
Make the room comfortable.

Consider:
- Clean, dry bedding
- Proper room temperature
- Soft lighting
- Minimal noise
- Positioning for comfort

Small adjustments can improve sleep quality.

---

## 10.3 Pain and Discomfort
Pain interferes with rest.

CNAs should:
- Ask about pain before bedtime
- Report complaints to the nurse
- Provide comfort measures like pillows and blankets
- Check for skin pressure areas

Timely reporting helps the nurse manage pain effectively.

---

## 10.4 Late-Night Care
Nighttime care should be gentle.

Best practices:
- Speak softly and move quietly
- Keep interactions brief but compassionate
- Protect privacy and comfort
- Leave the call light within reach
- Avoid bright lights if possible

Minimize disruption while meeting needs.

---

## 10.5 Sleep Routines
A consistent routine helps residents relax.

Good habits include:
- Regular bedtime schedule
- Calm activities before bed
- Limiting caffeine later in the day
- Encouraging fluid intake earlier in the evening
- Comforting reassurance for anxious residents

Consistent routines support better sleep.
