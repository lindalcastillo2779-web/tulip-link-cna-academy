# Chapter 2: Legal and Ethical Issues

## Learning Objectives
- Understand resident rights and how to protect them
- Recognize and report abuse and neglect
- Maintain resident confidentiality
- Apply ethical principles in care
- Understand HIPAA regulations
- Know reporting procedures

---

## 2.1 Resident Rights
Residents have the right to safe, respectful care. This includes:
- Privacy during all personal care
- Choice and participation in care decisions
- Confidential treatment of personal information
- Freedom from abuse and neglect
- The right to refuse care when able

Residents should be treated as individuals and given the opportunity to make informed decisions whenever possible.

---

## 2.2 Confidentiality and Privacy
A resident's medical and personal information is private. CNAs must:
- Protect health information from unauthorized disclosure
- Avoid sharing resident details in public areas
- Store records securely
- Discuss resident care only with the healthcare team

HIPAA requires that protected health information remain confidential. A CNA should never post private information on social media or share details with visitors unless authorized.

---

## 2.3 Abuse, Neglect, and Exploitation
Abuse and neglect can take many forms:
- Physical abuse: hitting, slapping, rough handling
- Emotional abuse: threats, humiliation, insults
- Neglect: failure to provide needed care, food, or assistance
- Financial exploitation: unauthorized use of money or property

If you suspect abuse or neglect, report it immediately to the nurse or supervisor. Document your observations accurately.

---

## 2.4 Ethical Decision-Making
Ethical care means doing what is right for the resident.

CNAs should:
- Respect resident preferences and values
- Support dignity and independence
- Be honest and trustworthy
- Stay within their scope of practice
- Seek guidance when uncertain

When care conflicts with a resident's request, discuss it with the nurse and follow the care plan.

---

## 2.5 Reporting Procedures
Report any concerns promptly and clearly.

Steps to report:
1. Tell the nurse immediately
2. Provide objective facts (what you observed, when, and where)
3. Document accurately in the resident's record
4. Follow facility policy for incident reports
5. Continue monitoring the resident
