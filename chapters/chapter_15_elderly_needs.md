# Chapter 15: Special Needs of the Elderly

## Learning Objectives
- Understand common aging changes
- Recognize chronic condition impacts
- Prevent falls and injuries in older adults
- Maintain dignity while providing care
- Adapt care for sensory changes
- Support independence safely

---

## 15.1 Common Age-Related Changes
Aging affects many body systems.

Common changes include:
- Thinner, drier skin
- Reduced strength and balance
- Slower reaction time
- Decreased vision and hearing
- Changes in memory and thinking
- Digestive changes

Knowing these changes helps CNAs provide better care.

---

## 15.2 Chronic Conditions
Many older adults manage long-term conditions.

Common issues include:
- Arthritis
- Diabetes
- Heart disease
- COPD
- Hypertension
- Dementia

CNAs should support medication schedules, mobility, and monitoring of symptoms.

---

## 15.3 Fall Risk
Older adults are at higher fall risk.

Risk factors:
- Weak muscles
- Poor vision
- Medications that cause dizziness
- Uneven surfaces
- Improper footwear

Prevent falls by clearing hazards, using assistive devices, and offering help consistently.

---

## 15.4 Sensory Changes
Hearing and vision changes can affect safety.

To help:
- Speak clearly and face the resident
- Use good lighting
- Reduce background noise
- Use large print or visual cues as needed
- Check hearing aids and glasses are in place

Accommodating senses improves communication and safety.

---

## 15.5 Respect and Dignity
Older residents deserve respect for their life experience.

Show respect by:
- Asking permission before care
- Respecting privacy and modesty
- Involving residents in care choices
- Listening to their preferences
- Valuing their stories and opinions

Respect preserves dignity and strengthens relationships.
