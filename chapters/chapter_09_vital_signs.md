# Chapter 9: Vital Signs

## Learning Objectives
- Measure temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure correctly
- Understand normal ranges for vital signs
- Recognize abnormal findings
- Position residents properly for measurements
- Report changes appropriately
- Document vital signs accurately

---

## 9.1 Temperature
Body temperature shows heat balance.

Common methods:
- Oral
- Axillary
- Tympanic
- Temporal

Record the exact reading and report fever or unusually low results.

---

## 9.2 Pulse
Pulse rate and rhythm reflect heart function.

To measure:
- Use the index and middle finger
- Count beats for 30 seconds and double it
- Observe strength and regularity
- Report rates outside the normal range

Normal adult pulse is 60-100 beats per minute.

---

## 9.3 Respiration
Respiration measures breathing.

To observe:
- Count breaths for 30 seconds while the resident is resting
- Watch chest rise and fall
- Do not tell the resident you are counting
- Note depth, rhythm, and effort

Normal respirations are 12-20 breaths per minute.

---

## 9.4 Blood Pressure
Blood pressure measures force against artery walls.

Key steps:
- Position the arm at heart level
- Use a bare arm
- Place the cuff snugly
- Identify systolic and diastolic sounds accurately
- Record the numbers exactly

Report any abnormal readings immediately.

---

## 9.5 Documentation and Reporting
Record vital signs as instructed.

Include:
- Exact numbers
- Date and time
- Resident position
- Any symptoms observed
- Notification to the nurse if abnormal

Accurate documentation supports safe nursing decisions.
