# Chapter 8: Nutrition and Hydration

## Learning Objectives
- Support proper nutrition and hydration
- Assist with safe feeding and swallowing
- Observe and report nutrition concerns
- Respect dietary preferences and restrictions
- Recognize signs of dehydration
- Document fluid and food intake

---

## 8.1 Nutrition Basics
Good nutrition supports healing and strength.

Remember:
- Offer balanced meals when possible
- Encourage intake of fruits, vegetables, protein, and fluids
- Respect special diets and allergies
- Report poor appetite or missed meals
- Observe tray return and meal completion

Nutrition affects energy, immunity, and mood.

---

## 8.2 Hydration
Fluids are essential for body function.

Hydration tips:
- Offer fluids frequently throughout the day
- Provide water, juice, or approved beverages
- Watch for signs of dehydration (dry mouth, low urine output)
- Encourage sipping if the resident dislikes large amounts at once
- Document fluid intake as directed

Proper hydration supports circulation, digestion, and temperature control.

---

## 8.3 Feeding Assistance
Assist residents with dignity.

When feeding:
- Position the resident upright if able
- Make sure the mouth is empty before the next bite
- Offer small spoonfuls and encourage chewing
- Respect dietary restrictions and preferences
- Never force food or fluids

Watch for coughing, choking, or refusal.

---

## 8.4 Swallowing Precautions
Some residents need special diets.

Common precautions:
- Thickened liquids
- Soft or pureed foods
- Small bites and slow pace
- Supervision during meals
- No straws if prohibited

Follow the care plan closely for safe swallowing.

---

## 8.5 Documentation and Reporting
Record intake carefully.

Include:
- Amount eaten and drank
- Refusals or signs of difficulty
- Any nausea, vomiting, or pain
- Changes in appetite or swallowing
- Weight changes if tracked

Accurate records help the healthcare team adjust care.
