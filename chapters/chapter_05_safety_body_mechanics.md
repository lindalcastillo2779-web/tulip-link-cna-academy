# Chapter 5: Patient Safety and Body Mechanics

## Learning Objectives
- Prevent falls and injuries in residents
- Use proper body mechanics to protect yourself
- Recognize environmental hazards
- Use equipment safely
- Respond to emergency situations
- Follow safety protocols

---

## 5.1 Fall Prevention
Falls are a leading cause of injury in long-term care.

Prevent falls by:
- Keeping pathways clear
- Locking bed and wheelchair wheels
- Making sure residents wear non-skid footwear
- Using call lights and answering them promptly
- Providing assistance during toileting and transfers

If a resident falls, do not move them until a nurse assesses the situation.

---

## 5.2 Proper Body Mechanics
Protect your back and joints by:
- Keeping your back straight
- Bending at your knees, not your waist
- Holding objects close to your body
- Using your legs to lift
- Avoiding twisting motions

Good mechanics help prevent caregiver injury and improve safety for residents.

---

## 5.3 Equipment Safety
Check equipment before use.

Ensure:
- Wheels are locked
- Breaks function correctly
- Gait belts are secure
- Assistive devices are in good condition
- Bed rails and side supports are positioned correctly

Report broken or unsafe equipment immediately.

---

## 5.4 Environmental Hazards
A safe environment is essential.

Watch for:
- Wet floors or spills
- Loose rugs or clutter
- Furniture blocking exits
- Poor lighting
- Tripping hazards

Cleaning or reporting hazards quickly helps prevent accidents.

---

## 5.5 Emergency Care and Disaster Preparedness
Know facility procedures for:
- Fires
- Code calls
- Severe weather
- Evacuations

Always stay calm, follow instructions, and support resident safety.
