# Chapter 12: Transfers and Mobility

## Learning Objectives
- Use proper body mechanics during transfers
- Apply safe transfer techniques
- Assist with ambulation and mobility
- Position residents properly
- Prevent complications from immobility
- Encourage resident independence when safe

---

## 12.1 Transfer Preparation
Preparation makes transfers safer.

Before transfer:
- Explain the procedure clearly
- Check resident readiness
- Position the wheelchair close to the bed
- Lock the bed and wheelchair wheels
- Apply a gait belt if required

Careful preparation reduces fall risk.

---

## 12.2 Using a Gait Belt
A gait belt provides support during transfers.

Use it by:
- Placing it around the resident's waist
- Securing it snugly but comfortably
- Holding the belt at the back or sides
- Encouraging the resident to help if possible
- Avoiding pulling the resident by the arms

The belt helps maintain balance and control.

---

## 12.3 Ambulation Assistance
Assist safely when residents walk.

During ambulation:
- Walk slightly behind and to the side
- Allow the resident to move at their own pace
- Watch for signs of fatigue or dizziness
- Use a walker or cane as prescribed
- Keep the path clear of obstacles

Support without taking over the resident's movement.

---

## 12.4 Positioning and Alignment
Proper positioning prevents discomfort and pressure injuries.

Ensure:
- Spines are aligned naturally
- Pillows support the head, shoulders, and legs
- Hips and knees are comfortable
- Feet are supported when sitting
- The resident is repositioned routinely

Good alignment helps breathing, circulation, and comfort.

---

## 12.5 Restorative Mobility
Encourage safe function.

Support residents by:
- Encouraging them to do what they can
- Practicing range-of-motion exercises as ordered
- Helping with standing and short walks
- Using assistive devices correctly
- Reporting weakness or balance issues

Restorative mobility focuses on independence, not only safety.
