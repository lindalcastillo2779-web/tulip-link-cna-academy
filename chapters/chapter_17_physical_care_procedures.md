# Chapter 17: Introduction to Physical Care Procedures

## Learning Objectives
- Make occupied beds safely and efficiently
- Perform range-of-motion exercises properly
- Position residents for comfort and safety
- Prevent pressure injuries with good care
- Assist with daily activities using proper technique
- Maintain infection prevention during procedures

---

## 17.1 Making Occupied Beds
Making a bed with a resident in it requires care.

Best practices:
- Explain each step to the resident
- Keep the resident covered as much as possible
- Roll the resident gently while changing linens
- Smooth sheets to prevent wrinkles
- Check for pressure points during repositioning

This procedure promotes comfort and cleanliness.

---

## 17.2 Range-of-Motion Exercises
Range-of-motion (ROM) exercises maintain joint flexibility.

Type of ROM:
- Active ROM: resident moves independently
- Passive ROM: caregiver moves the resident
- Active-assist ROM: resident helps with support

Move joints slowly and gently. Stop if the resident shows pain.

---

## 17.3 Positioning and Pressure Relief
Positioning prevents skin breakdown and supports comfort.

Guidelines:
- Use pillows to support body parts
- Keep the spine aligned
- Change position at least every two hours for high-risk residents
- Avoid friction and shearing when moving residents
- Check skin regularly, especially over bony areas

Good positioning reduces pressure injury risk.

---

## 17.4 Assistance with ADLs
Help residents with activities of daily living.

These include:
- Grooming
- Dressing
- Bathing
- Feeding
- Toileting

Allow the resident to do as much as they can safely.

---

## 17.5 Infection Prevention During Procedures
Always keep clean technique in mind.

Remember:
- Use hand hygiene before and after procedures
- Wear gloves when needed
- Keep equipment clean and dry
- Avoid touching soiled surfaces with clean hands
- Dispose of waste properly

Safe procedures protect residents and caregivers.
