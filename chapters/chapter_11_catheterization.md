# Chapter 11: Catheterization and Urinary Catheter Care

## Learning Objectives
- Understand types of urinary catheters
- Provide daily catheter care safely
- Prevent catheter-related infection
- Maintain proper drainage and positioning
- Recognize catheter complications
- Report problems promptly

---

## 11.1 Types of Catheters
Common catheters include:
- Indwelling Foley catheters
- Straight catheters
- Condom catheters
- Intermittent catheters

The nurse or physician orders the type and duration of use.

---

## 11.2 Daily Catheter Care
Good catheter care protects the resident.

Steps include:
- Hand hygiene before and after care
- Wearing gloves
- Keeping the catheter tubing secure
- Cleaning the insertion site gently
- Emptying the drainage bag as directed

Always avoid tugging on the catheter.

---

## 11.3 Drainage Bag Management
Proper bag positioning is essential.

Remember:
- Keep the bag below bladder level
- Avoid letting it touch the floor
- Empty the bag when it is two-thirds full
- Keep the collection port clean
- Do not disconnect tubing unless ordered

Correct positioning prevents backflow and infection.

---

## 11.4 Signs of Catheter Problems
Watch for:
- Cloudy or foul-smelling urine
- Leakage around the catheter
- Pain or discomfort
- Low urine output
- Redness at the insertion site

Report any concerns to the nurse right away.

---

## 11.5 Documentation
Record catheter care accurately.

Include:
- Time and amount of output
- Appearance of urine
- Condition of the catheter site
- Resident complaints or discomfort
- Any concerns reported to nursing staff

Accurate records support safe catheter management.
