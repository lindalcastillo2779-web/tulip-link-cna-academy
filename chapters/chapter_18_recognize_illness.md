# Chapter 18: Recognizing and Reporting Resident Illness

## Learning Objectives
- Recognize signs of acute illness
- Understand when vital signs are abnormal
- Report changes in condition promptly
- Document observations objectively
- Know when to call for emergency help
- Support residents during acute changes

---

## 18.1 Signs of Acute Illness
Changes in a resident's condition may signal illness.

Watch for:
- Sudden confusion or disorientation
- Changes in breathing or pulse
- Fever or chills
- Pain or discomfort
- Decreased appetite or intake
- Changes in skin color or temperature

Report these observations immediately to the nurse.

---

## 18.2 Vital Sign Changes
Vital signs help detect problems early.

Report if you observe:
- Temperature over normal range
- Rapid or irregular pulse
- Rapid or labored breathing
- High or low blood pressure
- Low oxygen saturation (if measured)

Always note the resident's position and activity when measured.

---

## 18.3 Documenting Observations
Record what you see and hear clearly.

Good documentation includes:
- The exact time of the observation
- What you observed without judgment
- Any symptoms the resident reports
- Actions you took or reported
- Who you notified

Objective documentation supports accurate nursing decisions.

---

## 18.4 Reporting Emergencies
Some situations require immediate action.

Call for help when you see:
- Severe chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Sudden weakness or paralysis
- Severe bleeding
- Loss of consciousness
- Signs of stroke

Follow facility emergency protocols and stay calm.

---

## 18.5 Supporting Residents During Acute Change
Continue compassionate care.

Do:
- Stay with the resident if it is safe to do so
- Keep them as comfortable as possible
- Explain what is happening in calm language
- Avoid unnecessary movement
- Update the nurse with any changes

Your calm presence can ease fear and confusion.
